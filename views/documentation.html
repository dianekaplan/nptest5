Setup Steps:

  1. In your friendly cs50 IDE instance, create a new directory
  2. Paste my submitted files into that new directory
  3. In phpMyAdmin, create new database for our tables to live
  4. Back in the code, update line 20 of project.sql to reflect the new database name (replace 'project' with what you called it)
  5. update config.json to use name of the database (note: this is case sensitive!), and your username/password credentials
  6. Start apache as usual: apache50 start ~/workspace/ [new_instance_name]/public
  7. Start mysql: mysql50 start
  8. Click the link in the cs50 IDE upper-right corner, and browser will load with the site!

Inspiration:

At least once a year I find myself travelling with no access to a gym and without a good/safe place to run, so I'm scrambling to improvise some kind of workout. It's usually the same boring mix of pushups, situps, lunges, and dips off the bed. Working out with November Project has shown me that you can get a solid workout without any equipment at all, especially if you have a little space, common stuff you find in the park, and best of all a willing buddy. With this tool, no more scrambling: a new workout is a couple clicks away.

Using this tool:

Make a new workout: pick what resources you have available. Leave the bottom-section defaults for a 15-20 minute workout, or play with the number of different sets, and exercises per set. Hit submit, and you're off! On your workout page, hover over the exercise name to see a description, or click thumbnail to see what it looks like. Everybody's ability is different. We give suggested timing and counts just as a placeholder- choose whatever feels like the right challenge for you.

Working out on your own, cycle through the exercises at your challenge level (1 minute each, 20 each, etc) continuing for a 4 minute set, 8 minute set, enough to get comfortable with the exercises and really feel it! If you're working out with a partner, you'll get pairs of exercises- sometimes working together and switching in the middle, and sometimes one partner travelling to a point and back, while the other does a different exercise on the spot (cheering partner to come back so they can switch!). You'll agree on the distance/time/duration together (e.g. to that tree and back) for what challenges you.

If you like workout you got, you can print it, facebook-share it, or email it to yourself. If you you want something different, click the links at the bottom of the page: get a new workout using the same stuff you selected before, or search fresh to make a change. You can also browse the exercise list to see what we've got in there so far, and email me if you have questions or ideas to make the tool better!

Enjoy!
